Academic Profile

Global Leadership

Currently serving as Regional Director (South Asia) for the International Nitrogen Initiative (INI). Dr. Aziz coordinates transboundary nitrogen management strategies across the South Asian subcontinent.

Institutional Role

Associate Professor at the Institute of Soil and Environmental Sciences, University of Agriculture, Faisalabad (UAF). Over 15 years of dedicated academic and field research in soil fertility.

Impact Metrics

Author of over 120 peer-reviewed articles in high-impact journals. Lead Editor for global assessments including "Nitrogen Assessment: Pakistan as a Case Study".

Managing Nitrogen for a Sustainable Future

Focused on improving fertilizer use efficiency and reducing nutrient pollution through innovative soil management practices.

Core Research Pillars

Nutrient Recycling

Developing protocols for the efficient recycling of nutrients from organic waste to enhance soil health and crop productivity.

Fertilizer Efficiency

Researching deep tillage and synchronized fertilizer application rates to minimize environmental leaching and maximize uptake.

Pollution Mitigation

Strategic lead in identifying nitrogen pollution hotspots and advising on policy-level mitigation in the South Asia Nitrogen Hub.
